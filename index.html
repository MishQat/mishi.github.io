Project Overview

This group Design‚ÄìMake‚ÄìTest project focused on developing a small, gravity-propelled vehicle capable of traversing a 5-metre inclined track using only the energy from a 2.5 kg falling mass.

The project explored the practical application of energy transfer, drivetrain optimisation, and real-world engineering trade-offs between performance, durability, and manufacturing cost.

My Role & Contributions

  • Designed the full vehicle assembly in CAD using premade component dimensions
  • Created the plywood chassis design to serve as the primary structural element
  • Imported and integrated standard components (bearings, fasteners, shafts)
  • Produced detailed engineering drawings for manufacturing and assembly

Engineering Challenges

A major constraint of the project was the use of predefined, industry-standard components, limiting geometric freedom while increasing the importance of accurate modelling and component selection.

The drivetrain and gearbox design required balancing torque and speed to ensure sufficient traction on the incline while preventing wheel slip and excessive energy loss through friction.

Design Approach

The design process was guided by theoretical modelling of energy conversion from gravitational potential energy to kinetic energy. A Python script was developed to rapidly evaluate different wheel sizes and gearbox ratios, enabling efficient optimisation of the drivetrain configuration.

Outcome

The final vehicle successfully met the project constraints, achieving reliable performance across multiple test runs while maintaining structural integrity and manufacturability.
